Explaining Portuguese exam results using LIME¶

The goal of this homework was to explain the results of students from their lifestyles and life situations. Similarly to the previous homework, I use the Portuguese dataset only. For explanations I use lime from the dalex module. I found that the values given by lime are fairly stable, though differ slightly for different seeds. The lime explanations are different from shap, but both seem reasonable - shap is global and lime local. The model that is being explained is mostly XGBoost with the exception of the last subtask, where explanations for XGBoost are compared with explanations from Random Forest model. There are some systematic differences between explanations for XGBoost and Random Forest models.

Subtask 3¶

Lime seems to be fairly stable in this case. The predictions for different seeds differ, but the most important variables remain most important. Their order might change a bit and also the magnitude of contribution.

For the student with id 405 and for seed 0, we get the explanations below. As we can see, the most important feature is having failed the class previously - it has a huge negative contribution. Then comes attending the better school and not receiving additional educational support from the school. And then there is negative contribution from drinking alcohol during weekdays.

We can see that with seed 2, drinking alcohol during weekdays has a bigger negative contribution. The contribution of father not being the teacher is smaller than for seed 0. Also mother's education and absences are more important in the second case - for seed 0 sex and reason for choosing school were more important.

Subtask 4¶

Let's now look at the shap results of the same student. Interestingly it looks completely different. Failures remain the most important negative contributor, but nearly everything else is different! So for instance here, not wanting to purse higher education is the second importan feature, but in the case of lime it didn't even make it to the list. In the case of shap, moderate alcohol consumption during weekdays is a big positive contributor, and in lime it is a big negative contributor. There are more differences amongst the rest of the variables too.

Subtask 5¶

There are some systematic differences between the explanations for XGBoost and Random Forest models. The first one is that for XGBoost the reason_other variable more important than for Random Forest. The meaning of this variable is whether a given student had other reason for selecting their school than distance to home, reputation and courses available. This is visible among more samples, but I present only one as a proof and the rest can be found in the appendix.

In [10]:
def encode_categorical(df):
    df = df.copy()
    
    df = df.replace({'GP': 0, 'MS': 1})
    df = df.replace({'F': 0, 'M': 1})
    df = df.replace({'U': 0, 'R': 1})
    df = df.replace({'LE3': 0, 'GT3': 1})
    df = df.replace({'A': 0, 'T': 1})
    df = df.replace({'no': 0, 'yes': 1})
    
    onehot_features = ['Mjob', 'Fjob', 'reason', 'guardian']
    df = pd.concat([df, pd.get_dummies(df[onehot_features])], axis=1)
    df = df.drop(onehot_features, axis=1)

    return df
